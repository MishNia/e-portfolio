Screencasting

About the Project

For my screencasting project, I created a 2-4 minute tutorial on how to use Trello to organize tasks and manage projects effectively. Trello is a popular tool for personal and team productivity, and this tutorial demonstrates its key features, including creating boards, adding lists and cards, and organizing tasks visually.


I used Camtasia’s free trial to record the screencast. First, I prepared a clear and concise script to ensure the video flowed smoothly and covered all essential points within the time limit. I then recorded my screen while narrating the steps in real time, showcasing each feature of Trello as I explained it.


After recording, I used Camtasia’s editing tools to enhance the tutorial and make it engaging:


Effects and Enhancements:

1. Zooming and Panning: I applied zoom and pan effects to emphasize important actions, such as clicking buttons or dragging cards.

2. Smart Focus: Camtasia’s smart focus feature helped keep the viewer’s attention on critical areas of the screen.

3. Cursor Highlighting: I used cursor highlighting to make my mouse movements more noticeable, ensuring clarity for learners.


Visual Additions:

1. I added a title card from Camtasia’s library at the start of the video to introduce the topic

2. At the end, I included a thank-you card 

3. I also added transitions that match the title and thank you cards


Audio Enhancements:

1. I paired my narration with peppy background music from Camtasia’s library to create a positive and engaging tone for the tutorial.

2. The audio levels were balanced to ensure the background music complemented the narration without overpowering it.
